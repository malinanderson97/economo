Interactive Macroeconomics Teaching Tool
Economo
A browser-based IS-LM-PC simulator for intermediate macro — drag the curves, step through time, watch the economy respond.
What it is. A single HTML file that runs in any browser — no install, no login, no internet needed. Open it and it works: project it in a lecture, embed it in your VLE, or email it to students. It turns the core models of intermediate macro into something students manipulate directly rather than read about.

One tool, four stages

The full model is always on screen. Complexity unlocks stage by stage, following the course syllabus rather than the textbook's chapter order — the exchange rate is introduced before inflation. There is one engine throughout, governed by two switches: the closed economy is simply the open economy with the trade channel switched off, and the nominal–real rate distinction (r = i − πᵉ) does not exist at all until the Phillips curve unlocks at stage 3. Before that the model runs r = i — Blanchard's fixed-price short run.

Why it helps students learn

Simultaneity, made visible

Drag the IS curve and the LM line, Phillips curve, and time path all move at once — the thing hardest to convey on a static whiteboard.

Diagram tied to algebra

A panel under each chart shows the live equation — Y = C + I + G (+ NX) with current numbers — so curve and identity update together.

Dynamics, not just equilibria

Step period by period to watch a shock land, prices adjust, and the economy return to potential — the short-run / medium-run story unfolding live.

The hard ideas, surfaced

The Taylor principle, expectations anchoring and de-anchoring, exchange-rate disinflation, the twin deficits — each has a ready-made scenario. The 1970s and 1990s presets are a matched pair: same shock, different credibility.

What's included

  • The full simulator, with six ready-to-run preset scenarios and teaching narratives.
  • An Instructor Manual with everything you need to get started.
  • A Model–Textbook Correspondence document mapping every equation, block by block, to Blanchard's 9th edition — with each one labelled exact, linearised, tool addition, or deviation. Where the tool departs from the textbook it says so and says why, so the model can be handed to colleagues and examiners without caveats.

Practical fit

  • No IT involvement. One file; works offline; nothing to install or maintain.
  • Course-ready. The stages and scenarios map onto standard intermediate-macro syllabi; usable in lectures, seminars, or as a self-study resource.
  • Transparent economics. Every coefficient is either derived from Blanchard's own building blocks or documented as a calibration or a deliberate deviation. Nothing is a black box, and nothing is quietly fudged.
